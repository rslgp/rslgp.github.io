SOLONE — Visual Design Brief

For: UI/UX Designer & VFX Artist
Game: SOLONE — Survive alone. How long can you last?
Engine: Defold (Lua) · Canvas: 640 × 1136 px · Target: Mobile + HTML5
Tone: Minimalist arcade with maximum juice. Every moment of play should feel crisp, readable, and satisfying to both the player and a spectator watching the screen.

1 · Design Philosophy

SOLONE is intentionally minimal in structure but rich in feedback. The entire game is dots on a black field. That constraint is a feature — every piece of art must earn its place by communicating game state, not by decorating. The design goal is juiciness: every kill, every hit absorbed, every powerup collected must feel unmissably good through layered simultaneous feedback (scale + color + light).

Core visual contract:
  • The player always knows their powerup state from their sprite color alone.
  • Every enemy death is a small celebration.
  • Every dangerous moment has a visual warning that fires before the danger lands.
  • Spectators watching the screen should be able to follow what is happening without playing.

3 · Sprites — What Exists & What Needs Replacing or Creating

All sprites are currently simple geometric shapes. Entries marked UPGRADE need replacement; entries marked NEW do not exist yet.

3.1 · Player

The player is the character the user controls. Single circular sprite, ~40 px diameter at 1× scale. It scales up to 2.3× (berserker) and squashes/bounces constantly in response to game events.

Key animation moments (code-driven)

  • Powerup pickup: 1.0 → 1.2 → 1.0 scale in ~150ms
  • Berserker entrance: scale overshoots to 2.3× with spring bounce
  • Protected hit: squash to 82%, spring back
  • Death: gray tint + any death-burst particle layer

4 · Particle Effects

All effects below are currently absent or minimal. Each represents a UX moment where more visual feedback would increase satisfaction.

4.1 · Kill Confirmation (highest priority)

When any enemy dies:

  • Burst: 6–10 tiny particles (enemy's current body color) fly outward radially, travel ~40px, fade in 200ms
  • Flash ring: white ring expands from death position, ~60px diameter at peak, fades in 150ms
  • Scale-out: sprite already does this; particle layer adds on top

4.2 · Powerup Collection Burst

  • Color burst: 8 tiny particles in powerup's color fly outward from player, 30px, fade in 120ms
  • Layered on top of player's existing scale burst for double-confirmation (transient + persistent)

4.3 · Powerup Expiry Flash

When a timed powerup expires (flame, turret, bullet_time, ghost, ironman, laser):

  • Orange pulse ring: ring expands from player, orange at 55% alpha, fades in 300ms
  • Particle ring makes it unmissable vs. current tint-only approach

4.4 · Milestone Kill Celebration

At kill counts 5, 10, 20, 50, 100:

  • Gold shower: 12–16 gold particles burst upward and fall, 400ms
  • Screen edge pulse: screen edges briefly pulse gold at 30% opacity for 150ms

4.5 · Bomb & Artillery Explosion

  • Inner flash: bright white circle expanding to explosion radius, fades in 100ms
  • Shockwave ring: white ring at edge of radius, expands 20% beyond, fades in 200ms
  • Debris: 8–12 tiny fragments fly outward, fade in 300ms

4.6 · Berserker Entry / Exit

  • Entry: green radial burst from player, 2.3× radius, 300ms — matches Hulk-transform feel
  • Exit: green particles implode toward player center as scale shrinks (not outward)

4.7 · Squeegee Screen Wipe

The squeegee is a large horizontal bar sweeping the screen. Enemies it touches should:

  • Flash white for 50ms
  • Leave brief white streak trail

The squeegee bar itself: leading edge glow (bright white 8px) and trailing smear (50% opacity, 20px fade).

4.8 · Death Balloon

Currently an emoji floats upward for 2.5s. Enhancements:

  • Slight wobble animation (±3° rotation, 0.4s cycle)
  • Optional: floating dust particles rising alongside

4.9 · Enemy Spawn Entrance

Currently a spring scale-in from 0. Add a brief white ring at the spawn position (80ms, expands to ~50px, fades) — signals "enemy appeared here".

10 · Spectator Experience — What an Observer Sees

SOLONE is played on a mobile screen that others may watch. Visual design should communicate game state to a spectator with no context.

What a spectator needs to read at a glance:
  1. Where is the player? → White glowing dot, always the brightest object
  2. Is the player in danger? → Enemy tints and proximity
  3. What powerup is active? → Player tint color (saturated enough to read at arm's length)
  4. Is the game going well? → Kill counter top-center, visible without squinting
  5. Is something exciting happening? → Particle bursts, explosions, chain lightning must register as events from 50cm away

Spectator-specific enhancements

  • Kill milestone celebrations (5/10/20/50/100) must be visible to someone not holding the phone
  • Bomb explosions: shockwave ring reaches 60% of screen width at max radius
  • Cascade chain lightning: each hop produces a visible arc/flash, not just a dot flying between enemies
  • Berserker: when player is in RAGE (green, 2.3×), it must be obvious that something exceptional is happening

13 · What NOT to Design

These are hard constraints. Respecting them is part of the brief.
  • No background art. Pure black. No grid, no texture, no vignette. The action is the visual.
  • No enemy health bars. Enemies die in one hit.
  • No damage numbers. Kill counter does all score communication.
  • No tutorial overlays. Game teaches through play.
  • No character faces or personalities. Everything is abstract geometry — circles, lines, arcs.(Except Emojis)
  • No persistent glow around the player. Player tint communicates powerup state; a permanent halo would reduce legibility of that signal.
  • No UI chrome during gameplay. Only kill counter and pause button appear during play. Nothing else.

14 · Deliverable Format

Sprites

  • PNG with transparency (alpha channel required for all game objects)
  • 1× and 2× versions (e.g. sprite.png at 64×64, sprite@2x.png at 128×128)
  • Atlas-compatible: no bleeding edges; 2px transparent padding on all sides
  • Defold atlas naming: use in-game ID (e.g. player, enemy_base, powerup_flame, fx_kill_burst, btn_play_normal)

Particle frames

  • A spritesheet (single PNG, uniform grid) OR
  • Individual numbered frames (e.g. fx_kill_burst_01.png … fx_kill_burst_08.png)
